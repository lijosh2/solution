Solution to Yvonne & Josh’s Wedding Puzzle!

Writers: Yvonne C, Josh L; Testsolvers: Baskaran S

Each image in the puzzle is a close up of a board game, with one letter missing from the name of the game. For example, the first image is of [M]onopoly, with M as the missing letter.

Substituting the missing letters in each numbered clue produces a partially completed ditloid (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ditloid), with a single letter omitted from the ditloid and indicated by the red underscore. For example, looking at clue 4, the names of the games are Ticket To Ri[d]e, [L]ife and Cards Against Humanit[y]. Substituting the missing letters D, L and Y produces the ditloid: 29 D in _ (in a L Y).

Now we can solve each partially completed ditloid by inferring the the missing letters indicated by the red underscore. For example in clue 4, we can infer the complete ditloid is 29 D in F (in a L Y), which stands for “29 days in February (in a leap year)”.

The box at the end of the puzzle indicates that we need to concatenate the red underscored letters from each ditloid, in order. That tells us what guests need to do to solve this puzzle: SELFIE WITH COUPLE, i.e. take a selfie with the wedding couple!
